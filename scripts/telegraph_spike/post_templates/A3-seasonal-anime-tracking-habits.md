# Sustainable Habits for Seasonal Anime Tracking

Anime seasons run four times a year and each season ships dozens of new shows. A casual viewer who tries to "watch everything" burns out by week three. A more sustainable approach treats seasonal tracking as a filtering problem, not a completion problem.

## The three-pass filter

A useful pattern is to make three passes over the season's catalogue.

**Pass one** (premiere week): skim trailers and synopses, drop everything that doesn't promise something the viewer specifically wants. The aim here is to shrink the list to roughly 5–8 candidates, not to make final choices.

**Pass two** (episode 3): apply the standard "three-episode rule" — shows that haven't earned attention by episode three rarely improve enough to justify staying. Expect to drop half the list at this checkpoint.

**Pass three** (mid-season): a final cull at episode 6 or 7. By this point the remaining shows are ones the viewer is genuinely engaged with; everything else has either hit a slump or quietly become a chore.

## Index-driven tracking

Manual filtering scales poorly. Most experienced viewers rely on a stable index — a backlog database with synopsis, score, and runtime — to short-circuit the first pass entirely. A consolidated index like the [community ACG hub]({{TARGET_URL}}) covers premiere weeks for most major studios and saves the bulk of the upfront skim work.

The point of all this filtering isn't gatekeeping — it's protecting the time budget that makes anime enjoyable instead of a backlog chore.
